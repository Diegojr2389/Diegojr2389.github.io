Project Selection and Motivation

The idea for my capstone project came from one day speaking to a relative of mine, and them telling me that they would not be comfortable going to the gym due to them not knowing how to do weightlifting exercises correctly. They told me they would feel a bit embarrassed of performing the exercises incorrectly in front of lots of people. I then thought about how I could help them and, in the process, also help other people. That is when I pondered and came up with the idea of creating an app that they and others could use in order to correctly do an exercise comfortably, with proper form, without the prying eyes of others.

Throughout the project I stayed motivated for several reasons, one of them obviously being, to help my relative. Apart from that reason, I was also motivated by the fact that this would be the first large project I would be working on and I would be very proud of myself if I achieved what I was planning to do from the start. Another motivating factor was that this project also aligns with my future goals and aspirations.

I want to be a successful software engineer that works on projects that help thousands of people. May that be by building web apps, mobile apps, or other technologies, as long as I am creating solutions that end up having a meaningful impact on people's lives, I will always have a reason to stay motivated when working on any software engineering project such as this one.

Research and External Information

Throughout the project, I heavily relied on outside resources in order to improve the quality and academic grounding of my work. Since I had never developed an Android application before this capstone project, I relied on documentation, tutorials and AI tools in order to build my first android application and to troubleshoot issues that arose during development.

I also reached out to an expert, who provided valuable guidance on technical and design considerations, including privacy concerns when handling camera data, potential approaches for training machine learning models, and strategies for managing the scope of the project. Their guidance made me realize that not everything has to be fully automated: Hardcode logic is fine as long as it supports the overall objective. They taught me about the concept of timeboxing, which helped me when I was stuck on developing the real-time form feedback feature of my app due to the tools I had been using. He also gave me some pointers related to machine learning which will definitely help me with the implementation of the chatbot feature of my app.

Successes and Challenges

The biggest success of my project was implementing the real-time pose feedback feature of my application. I spent significant time troubleshooting, experimenting with different approaches, and reviewing documentations and tutorials in order to make the feature a reality, so being able to implement the feature was extremely rewarding.

The biggest challenge I ran into throughout the project was not being able to set up the Pose Detection API in my project. I was initially using MediaPipe with React Native, but I quickly ran into compatibility and configuration issues that prevented the API from functioning correctly. After lots of troubleshooting and constant back and forth, I reconsidered my approach and switched to Mobile development using Android Studio, Kotlin and from MediaPipe to the ML Kit Pose API. Although the setback delayed my progress, it ultimately led to smoother development and led to me being able to successfully continue the development of my app.

Reflection & Improvements

Looking back at my project, I realize that I could have done a lot of things differently. For one, If I had Timeboxing in my repertoire since the beginning, I would have spent a fixed amount of time evaluating potential solutions which might have directed me towards Android Development and ML Kit Pose more quickly. On that note, I should have contacted my expert much sooner. They would have introduced me to topics such as timeboxing and other helpful development strategies, earlier in the process, potentially helping me avoid the development obstacles that I encountered.

Thinking about the time frame, if I had more time I think I would have also trained my own landmark detection model instead of using the ML Kit Pose API. It would have been much more effective to give feedback to the users of my app. I say this because, the ML Kit Pose API has 33 fixed landmarks, not including landmarks on the spine. These landmarks are crucial since exercises like the squat and the deadlift need your back to be straight in order to prevent serious injuries.

Conclusion

After the extensive work on my project, I found it to be something worth building and spending my time on. Not only will it help others, but it has already helped me gain valuable experience in mobile development, real-time pose detection, working with APIs, troubleshooting complex technical issues, and improving my project planning and time management skills.

Looking at the future of my app, I plan to make my app more scalable and more visually appealing. I plan to train an AI chatbot that can answer questions my users might have about weightlifting, or other exercise related questions. I also plan to make my own pose detection model in order to give more helpful feedback to my users including landmarks for the spine and maybe other landmarks I find to be helpful in giving better feedback. But most importantly, I plan to develop my app for IOS, in order for more people to use it.
