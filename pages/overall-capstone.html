Project Selection and Motivation

The idea for my capstone project came from one day speaking to a relative of mine, and them telling me that they would not be comfortable going to the gym due to them not knowing how to do weightlifting exercises correctly. They told me they would feel a bit embarrassed of performing the exercises incorrectly in front of lots of people. Hearing this made me think about how many people might feel the same way, and so, I started wondering if there was a way I could help.

That moment became the starting point to my project. The idea I had in mind was for people to practice weightlifting exercises, comfortably, in a private environment. From this idea, I ended up developing the concept of a mobile app that gives the user real-time feedback on their form for different weightlifting exercises. To ultimately, help them build confidence without the pressure of being watched.

As the project progressed, I was able to stay motivated for several reasons. I was driven not only by the desire of helping my relative, but also by the opportunity of taking on a large-scale project for the first time. I knew that if I put in lots of effort and successfully completed the project, it would be something I could be proud of. At the same time, this project aligned with my future goals and aspirations: to become a successful software engineer who builds meaningful applications that positively impact the lives of thousands, if not millions of people.

Research and External Information

Because this was my first time developing an android application, I knew that I would have to rely on lots of external resources. I spent a significant amount of time looking through documentations, tutorials and AI tools in order to troubleshoot issues that arose during development.

In addition to these resources, I reached out to an expert, who provided me with valuable guidance. They gave me insights on technical and design considerations, such as privacy concerns when handling camera data and strategies for managing the scope of a project. An important lesson I learned was that not everything has to be fully automated; sometimes simpler, hardcoded logic is fine as long as it supports the overall objective.

They also introduced me to the concept of timeboxing, which became especially useful when I was stuck on developing the real-time form feedback feature of my app. Instead of trying to fix the same issue over and over again, I learned to step back, set limits, and rethink my approach. Their advice not only helped me overcome immediate challenges, but it also gave me strategies I can continue to use in future projects.

Successes and Challenges

One of the most rewarding moments of the project came when I was finally able to implement the real-time pose feedback feature of my application. I spent significant time troubleshooting, experimenting with different approaches, and reviewing documentations and tutorials in order to make the feature a reality. There were many moments throughout the project where progress felt slow, but seeing the feature work in real time made all of the effort worthwhile.

However, this success came after overcoming the biggest challenge I ran into during the project. Early on, I tried setting up MediaPipe, a Pose Detection API with React Native, but I quickly ran into compatibility and configuration issues that prevented the API from functioning correctly. After lots of troubleshooting and constant back and forth attempts to fix the problem, I was still unable to get the API working.

At that point, I decided to step back and reconsider my approach. After lots of thinking, I transitioned from React Native to android development using Android Studio and Kotlin. As for the APIs, I switched from MediaPipe to ML Kit Pose. Although the setback delayed my progress, it ultimately led to smoother development and led to me being able to successfully continue the development of my app.

Reflection & Improvements

Looking back at my project, I realize that I could have done a lot of things differently. For one, If I had Timeboxing in my repertoire since the beginning, I would have spent a fixed amount of time evaluating potential solutions which might have directed me towards Android Development and ML Kit Pose more quickly. On that note, I should have contacted my expert much sooner. They would have introduced me to topics such as timeboxing and other helpful development strategies, earlier in the process, potentially helping me avoid the development obstacles that I encountered.

Thinking about the time frame, if I had more time I think I would have also trained my own landmark detection model instead of using the ML Kit Pose API. It would have been much more effective to give feedback to the users of my app. I say this because, the ML Kit Pose API has 33 fixed landmarks, not including landmarks on the spine. These spine landmarks are crucial since exercises like the squat and the deadlift need your back to be straight in order to prevent serious injuries. A custom model could provide more helpful feedback, and ultimately, provide more value to the users of my app.

Conclusion

After the extensive work on my project, I found it to be something worth building and spending my time on. Not only will it help others, but it has already helped me gain valuable experience in mobile development, real-time pose detection, working with APIs, troubleshooting complex technical issues, and improving my project planning and time management skills.

Looking at the future of my app, I plan to make my app more scalable and more visually appealing. I plan to train an AI chatbot that can answer questions my users might have about weightlifting, or other exercise related questions. I also plan to make my own pose detection model in order to give more helpful feedback to my users including landmarks for the spine and maybe other landmarks I find to be helpful in giving better feedback. Finally, I plan to expand the app to IOS, so that it can reach a wider audience and help more people.
